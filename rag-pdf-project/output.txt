Answer:
The recommended chunking strategies for RAG pipelines include fixed-size character windows, sentence or paragraph-level chunking, and recursive character text splitters. Fixed-size character windows are simple but may split sentences mid-thought. Sentence or paragraph-level chunking respects linguistic boundaries but produces variable-length chunks. Recursive character text splitters attempt to split on paragraph, then sentence, then word boundaries, balancing coherence and uniformity. Additionally, chunk overlap—repeating tokens at boundaries—helps preserve context across adjacent chunks.
